Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our insurance plans and benefits.

How do I choose the right plan for my family?

Consider your family's medical needs, budget, and preferred healthcare providers. Our Standard Plan offers the best balance of coverage and cost for most families, but you can compare all plans in detail on this page or speak with one of our representatives to get personalized guidance.

Can I change my plan after enrollment?

Yes, you can change your plan during the annual open enrollment period or if you experience a qualifying life event such as marriage, birth of a child, or loss of other coverage. Contact our customer service team to learn more about your options.

Are pre-existing conditions covered?

Yes, all of our plans cover pre-existing conditions without waiting periods. We believe in providing comprehensive coverage regardless of your medical history. However, premium rates may vary based on your health condition assessment.

How do I find in-network providers?

You can search for in-network providers through our online provider directory or mobile app. You can filter by specialty, location, and availability to find the right healthcare professional for your needs. Our customer service team can also help you find providers in your area.

How do I submit a claim?

You can submit claims through our online portal, mobile app, or by mail. To submit online:

  1. Log in to your account
  2. Navigate to the Claims section
  3. Click "New Claim" and follow the instructions
  4. Upload any required documentation
Most claims are processed within 7-10 business days.

How does payment for services work?

For in-network providers, you'll typically pay your copay at the time of service, and the provider will bill us directly for the remainder. For out-of-network providers, you may need to pay the full amount upfront and submit a claim for reimbursement according to your plan's benefits.

Is there coverage while traveling internationally?

Our Premium Plan offers worldwide coverage for emergency medical services. Standard and Basic Plans provide emergency coverage within East Africa only. For any international travel, we recommend reviewing your coverage and considering supplemental travel insurance for comprehensive protection.

How does prescription drug coverage work?

Prescription drugs are covered based on our formulary, which categorizes medications into tiers:

  • Tier 1: Generic medications (lowest copay)
  • Tier 2: Preferred brand-name medications
  • Tier 3: Non-preferred brand-name medications
  • Tier 4: Specialty medications
Your specific coverage and copays depend on your selected plan.

Can I add dependents to my plan?

Yes, you can add eligible dependents including your spouse, children under 26 years old, and in some cases, other qualifying family members. Dependents can be added during initial enrollment, annual open enrollment, or within 30 days of a qualifying life event (such as birth, adoption, or marriage).

How can I contact customer support?

Our customer support team is available through multiple channels:

  • Phone: +256-77183-0421-HEALTH-PLUS (Monday-Friday, 8am-8pm)
  • Email: support@healthcareplus.com
  • Live Chat: Available on our website and mobile app
  • In Person: Visit one of our local offices
For urgent matters outside business hours, our 24/7 nurse helpline is available to all members.

What preventive services are covered?

All our plans cover a wide range of preventive services at no additional cost, including:

  • Annual wellness exams
  • Immunizations and vaccines
  • Cancer screenings (breast, cervical, colorectal)
  • Blood pressure and cholesterol screenings
  • Diabetes screenings
  • Prenatal care
Preventive care is essential for early detection and maintaining good health.

Do you offer telehealth services?

Yes, all our plans include 24/7 telehealth services. You can connect with healthcare providers via video or phone for non-emergency conditions like:

  • Cold and flu symptoms
  • Allergies
  • Skin conditions
  • Minor infections
  • Prescription refills
  • Mental health consultations
Telehealth services are available through our mobile app or website with minimal wait times.

What pediatric services are covered?

Our plans include comprehensive pediatric services:

  • Well-child visits and immunizations
  • Developmental screenings
  • Vision and hearing tests
  • Dental care (Premium Plan)
  • Treatment for illnesses and injuries
  • Specialist consultations
  • Behavioral health services
We're committed to providing quality healthcare for children at every stage of development.

Are surgeries covered by your plans?

Yes, all our plans cover medically necessary surgeries, including:

  • Inpatient hospital surgeries
  • Outpatient procedures
  • Diagnostic surgical procedures
  • Emergency surgeries
Coverage details, deductibles, and co-insurance amounts vary by plan. Prior authorization may be required for non-emergency procedures. Our Standard and Premium Plans offer more comprehensive surgical coverage with lower out-of-pocket costs.

What is the out-of-pocket maximum?

The out-of-pocket maximum is the most you'll pay for covered services in a plan year. After reaching this amount, we cover 100% of eligible expenses for the rest of the year. This provides financial protection against high medical costs.

  • Basic Plan: $8,000 individual / $16,000 family
  • Standard Plan: $5,500 individual / $11,000 family
  • Premium Plan: $3,000 individual / $6,000 family
Deductibles, copayments, and coinsurance all count toward your out-of-pocket maximum.
